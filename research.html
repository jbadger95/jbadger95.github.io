Research Summary

My research interests lie in developing and applying computational and mathematical tools to solve real world problems. I am currently working with Prof. Leszek Demkowicz to develop scalable high-order hp-adaptive finite element tools for modeling complex large scale systems. Thus far my work has included developing fast-integration routines and a parallel hp-adaptive solver for general discontinuous Petrov-Galerkin (DPG) finite element systems.

My diverse undergraduate research experience includes work in surface mechanics and curved folding, advanced radiation modeling, and acoustic scattering. Throughout my academic career I have been privileged to work with leading experts and talented researchers. I have stood on the shoulders of giants and cannot understate the impact of numerous mentors and collaborators on my academic and personal development.

  • Transverse Modal Instability Modeling in Optical Amplifier Fiber

    Optical amplifiers are ubiquitous in communication infrastructure and are fundamental to a number of technologies. However, the development of high-power fibers has largely stalled in the last decade due to the onset of transverse modal instability (TMI), a non-linear effect that causes signal degredation at certain mean power thresholds. This project with the Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL) seeks to understand TMI through use of large-scale computational modeling. Our current models consider two electromagnetic fields (signal and pump) coupled non-linearly to a thermal field. It is estimated that between 2000 and 10000 wavelengths of the fiber need to be modeled in 3D to observe TMI, making this problem intractable for many classical numerical methods.

    My work during the summer of 2019 developed sum factorization routines for fast-integration of prismatic-element matrices. The algorithms I developed and implemented reduced element assembly costs by a factor of 30 and allowed for model scale to be increased from 200 wavelengths to 2000 wavelengths. We hope these models will help to better understand TMI and lead to the development of higher-power optical amplifiers that will enable a host of new technologies.
